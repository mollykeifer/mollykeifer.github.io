Is Alex Ovechkin the greatest first overall drafted since 2000?

Author

Molly Keifer

Published

May 5, 2024

In the Washington Capitals’ 49 completed seasons, they have qualified for the Stanley Cup playoffs 33 times. Out of Alex Ovechkin’s 19 seasons in the NHL, the Capitals’ have made the playoffs 15 times. However, they have not won a series since winning the Stanley Cup in 2018.

Alex Ovechkin, captain of the Washington Capitals, was drafted first overall into the NHL in 2004. Prior to the NHL, Ovechkin played in the Russian Superleague for Dynamo Moscow from 2001 to 2005, starting at the age of 16. There he had 36 goals and 32 assists in 152 career games.

Ovechkin is already considered one of the greatest goal-scorer’s of the 21st century as he has a chance to pass the great Wayne Gretzky for No. 1 in NHL history. In fact, this past season he passed Gretzky for the most road goals in NHL history (403) and scored his 57th empty-net goal to pass Gretzky for first in NHL history.

Sidney Crosby, captain of the Pittsburgh Penguins, was drafted first overall into the NHL in 2005. As the Penguins and Capitals have been team rivals for decades, a player rivalry began in 2006 when Crosby and Ovechkin debuted as young talents.

Crosby led the NHL in goals in 2009-10 (51) and 2016-17 (44). And in 2016 Wayne Gretzky said Crosby “has proven over and over that he’s the nest player in the game today.” Crosby also joined Ovechkin in 2022 as the only active NHL players to score 500 goals.

In April of 1999 Gretzky announced his retirement from the NHL and fans began to wonder; is Alex Ovechkin the greatest first overall drafted since 2000? And where does Crosby stand in all of this?

Let’s take a look at some numbers.

Where does Alex Ovechkin stand in career goals compared to others?

Here’s the top five leaders in goals in the NHL since the 1979 season, when Wayne Gretzky debuted.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(gt)
library(ggrepel)

alltimeleaders <- read_csv("nhl_players_skater_stats_8023.csv") |>
  group_by(player) |>
  arrange(season) |>
  mutate(cummulative_goals = cumsum(g))

gretzky <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Wayne Gretzky")
ovechkin <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Alex Ovechkin")
crosby <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Sidney Crosby")
gartner <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Mike Gartner")
hull <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Brett Hull")
jagr <- alltimeleaders |> filter (player == "Jaromír Jágr")

top5 <- alltimeleaders |>
  group_by(player) |>
  arrange(desc(cummulative_goals)) |>
  slice(1) |>
  ungroup() |>
  top_n(5, wt=cummulative_goals)

ggplot() + 
  geom_step(data=alltimeleaders, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="light grey") +
  geom_step(data=gretzky, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="#0038A8") +
  geom_step(data=ovechkin, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="#C8102E") +
  geom_step(data=gartner, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="#FFFF00") +
  geom_step(data=hull, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="#006847") +
  geom_step(data=jagr, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, group=player), color="#FFB81C") +
  geom_point(data=top5, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals)) +
  geom_text_repel(data=top5, aes(x=season, y=cummulative_goals, label=player), nudge_y = .1, nudge_x = .2) +
  labs(
   x="", 
   y="Cummulative Goals", 
   title="Alex Ovechkin hangs out with other greats", 
   subtitle="The Capitals' forward is the only first overall draft pick after 2000 who's numbers are similar to Wayne Gretzky.",
   caption="Source: Hockey Reference | Molly Keifer"
  ) +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 20, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

Ovechkin is the only player who was drafted first overall after 2000 who is in the top five for career goals. In fact, he sits at No. 3 out of the five with 822 goals and in 2022 he became just the third player in NHL history to score 800 goals.

Who are the two guys in front of him? Wayne Gretzky with 917 goals and Jaromír Jágr with 830 goals.

But what about the offensive players drafted first round since 2000? Where does he rank when it comes to his career shooting percentage?

Ovechkin sits in the middle of the pack with a 13.1 shooting percentage. However, he has over 100 more shots per season compared to almost everyone with a higher shooting percentage than him. And what does more shots per season mean? More room for error that can create a lower shooting percentage.

Crosby’s shooting percentage is better by 1.3% but has shot 143 times less than Ovechkin.

Where does Ovechkin in stand career points compared to the same group?

Code 
allplayers <- read_csv("nhl_players_skater_stats_8023.csv")

firstroundersandleaders <- c("Connor Bedard", "Juraj Slafkovsky", "Alexis Lafrenière", "Jack Hughes", "Nico Hischier", "Auston Matthews", "Connor McDavid", "Nathan MacKinnon", "Nail Yakupov", "Ryan Nugent-Hopkins", "Taylor Hall", "John Tavares", "Steven Stamkos", "Patrick Kane", "Sidney Crosby", "Alex Ovechkin", "Rick Nash", "Ilya Kovalchuk", "Wayne Gretzky", "Jaromír Jágr","Mike Gartner", "Brett Hull")

points <- allplayers |>
  filter (pos != "D") |>
  group_by(player) |>
  summarise(
    totalgoals = sum(g),
    totalassists = sum(a)
  )

firstroundleaderpoints <- points |> filter(player %in% firstroundersandleaders)

firstroundleaderpointswide <- firstroundleaderpoints |> 
  pivot_longer(
    cols=starts_with("total"), 
    names_to="Type", 
    values_to="a")

alex <- firstroundleaderpointswide |> filter(player == "Alex Ovechkin") 

ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=firstroundleaderpointswide, aes(x=reorder(player, a), weight=a, fill=Type)) + 
  geom_bar(data=alex, aes(x=player, weight=a, fill= Type), color= "black") + 
  coord_flip() +
  scale_fill_manual(values=c("lightgrey","#C8102E"), labels=c("Total Assists", "Total Goals"), name="Career Numbers") +
  labs(
   x="", 
   y="", 
   title="Sidney Crosby surpasses Alex Ovechkin in points", 
   subtitle="The Penguins' star has nearly 300 more assists than Ovechkin, giving him 17 more points.", 
   caption="Source: Hockey Reference | Molly Keifer"
  ) +
  theme_minimal() + 
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 17, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 10), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

Through 1,426 games, Ovechkin has a total of 822 goals and 663 assists - totaling 1,485 career points. The first overall pick for the 2005 NHL Draft, Sidney Crosby, has a total of 550 goals and 952 assists - totaling 1,502 career points, while only playing 1,272 games.

Alex Ovechkin may be behind Sidney Crosby in points, but only has Wayne Gretzky to catch on the NHL’s all-time goal list, making him one of the greatest first overall drafted since 2000.
